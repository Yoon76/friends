Class Clown

Every AP Bio class, T-Bone shows up ready to perform for an audience that did not ask for a show. He confuses the room going silent with the room being entertained. They are not the same thing.

📏

Vertically Challenged

Standing at a height that generously rounds up to 5'1", T-Bone shares this curse with the legendary Jasper. The main difference is Jasper knows he's short. T-Bone still hasn't figured it out.

😐

Laughed At, Not With

There is a critical distinction between being the funny one and being the funny-looking one. T-Bone has never been within zip code of the former. Every laugh he hears is a verdict, not a reward.

🏈

Terrible Sports Opinions

Somehow managed to pledge loyalty to both the Cincinnati Reds and the Las Vegas Raiders — two franchises that exist to teach children about grief. His hot takes are served cold, wrong, and confidently.

The Mrs. Jones Files

Mrs. Jones has taught AP Biology for years. She has seen struggling students, distracted students, and students who simply needed more time. T-Bone is a category she had to invent.

She does not dislike him as a person. She dislikes what he does to her classroom, her lesson plan, her will to teach, and her blood pressure. Every time he raises his hand she takes a breath and prepares for the worst. The worst always delivers.

She has stopped calling on him voluntarily. She has started ending class slightly early to avoid the questions. She once looked directly at him and said "I don't know why you're in this class" and then moved on without waiting for an answer. Neither did he.

Unanimous Friend Group Verdict

The Cutie Patootie

Here is the thing about T-Bone that nobody talks about enough. Despite everything — the bad sports takes, the AP Bio disasters, the Mrs. Jones incidents — the guy is, objectively, adorable. It is deeply annoying. It should not be allowed.

He is the cutie patootie of the friend group and the whole friend group knows it. You cannot stay fully mad at him. He says something clueless and you are halfway through being disappointed and then he does that thing with his face and suddenly you are fine. It is a superpower he has no idea he has.

Small, chaotic, somehow charming. Even Mrs. Jones probably goes home and thinks "okay but he wasn't the worst." She would never say it. But she thinks it.
